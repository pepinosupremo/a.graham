Artist Statement

As an artist my works are deeply rooted in the lush beauty of the Ozark Mountains in western Arkansas. As I have matured, I have been fortunate to travel throughout our country, as well as other countries around the world. I discovered an innate sense of purpose between people and place. Life and art are both complex. I find paved roads, bridges, buildings, road signs, and other manmade structures to be viewed as symbols. These symbols show a particular aspect of how humans interact with the world in ways that are simultaneously beneficial and destructive. There is a feeling of beauty and a feeling of melancholy, all in the name of human advancements. Historically, I understand human suffering, famine, and disease, however I can see progression, love, and beauty. This is the nucleus of what I create.

My work encompasses a range of media including drawing, painting, printmaking, and mixed media. These techniques allow me to express the relationships between people, structures, and locations while simultaneously creating artwork that is visually captivating. I want my audience to view my artwork as personal, but with a profound feeling that it is theirs to experience as well.
